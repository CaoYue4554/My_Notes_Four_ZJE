组蛋白修饰和染色质调控的结构生物学：实验工作流程

## 一、样品制备和蛋白质纯化

1. **重组蛋白表达**
   - 将编码SAGA复合物组分(如Gcn5、Ada2、Ada3)的基因克隆到表达载体中
   - 转化到大肠杆菌表达菌株(BL21(DE3))
   - 使用IPTG诱导蛋白表达
   - 收集细胞并在天然条件下制备裂解液
   - 使用亲和层析(His标签、GST标签)纯化蛋白
   - 通过离子交换和分子筛层析进一步纯化

2. **核小体组装**
   - 表达并纯化重组组蛋白(H2A、H2B、H3、H4)
   - 从纯化的组蛋白中重折叠组蛋白八聚体
   - 制备含有核小体定位序列的DNA片段
   - 通过盐梯度透析重构核小体
   - 用天然PAGE验证组装

3. **引入组蛋白修饰**
   - 化学合成修饰的组蛋白
   - 使用纯化的HATs/HDACs进行酶促修饰
   - 使用天然化学连接法整合修饰的组蛋白尾部
   - 通过质谱和Western blot验证修饰

4. **复合物形成**
   - 将纯化的SAGA复合物与修饰的核小体结合
   - 优化缓冲条件以形成稳定复合物
   - 通过凝胶过滤层析验证复合物形成
   - 通过分析型超速离心评估化学计量比

## 二、结构分析

1. **冷冻电镜样品制备和数据收集**
   - 将3-4μl纯化的复合物应用于放电处理的电镜网格上
   - 吸除多余液体并在液态乙烷中快速冷冻
   - 筛选网格以评估冰质量和颗粒分布
   - 在电子显微镜(如Titan Krios)上收集高分辨率数据
   - 使用直接电子探测器记录电影
   - 使用运动校正软件处理数据

2. **冷冻电镜数据处理**
   - 进行CTF估计和校正
   - 使用自动和手动拾取选择颗粒
   - 提取颗粒并进行2D分类
   - 生成初始3D模型
   - 通过迭代分类优化3D结构
   - 应用后处理以提高分辨率
   - 使用傅里叶壳相关验证分辨率

3. **X射线晶体学(针对特定结构域)**
   - 筛选单个结构域的结晶条件
   - 优化晶体生长参数
   - 收获晶体并准备衍射
   - 在同步加速器设施收集衍射数据
   - 处理衍射数据并进行相位确定
   - 构建和优化原子模型
   - 验证模型质量

4. **模型构建和优化**
   - 将原子模型拟合到冷冻电镜密度图中
   - 根据实验数据优化模型
   - 验证模型几何形状和与密度的拟合
   - 分析蛋白质-蛋白质和蛋白质-核小体界面

## 三、生化和功能表征

1. **体外HAT/HDAC活性测定**
   - 准备底物(含未修饰组蛋白的核小体)
   - 与纯化的SAGA复合物或HDACs孵育
   - 通过以下方法检测乙酰化/去乙酰化:
     * 放射性标记乙酰辅酶A的掺入
     * 使用修饰特异性抗体进行Western blot
     * 质谱分析
   - 确定酶动力学参数(Km, Vmax)

2. **结合亲和力测量**
   - 等温滴定量热法(ITC)测量热力学参数
   - 表面等离子体共振(SPR)测量结合动力学
   - 荧光各向异性测量较小结构域的相互作用
   - 比较修饰与未修饰底物的结合亲和力

3. **突变分析**
   - 设计关键相互作用界面的突变
   - 使用定点突变产生突变蛋白
   - 纯化突变蛋白并评估结构完整性
   - 测量对结合和酶活性的影响
   - 与结构观察相关联

## 四、细胞和基因组研究

1. **细胞系工程**
   - 产生SAGA组分突变的酵母菌株
   - 使用CRISPR-Cas9编辑HATs/HDACs创建人类细胞系
   - 开发表达组蛋白突变体的细胞系(如K→R以防止乙酰化)
   - 通过Western blot和质谱验证修饰

2. **染色质免疫沉淀(ChIP)**
   - 在活细胞中将蛋白质与DNA交联
   - 将染色质超声处理至适当片段大小
   - 使用以下抗体进行免疫沉淀:
     * 修饰的组蛋白(如H3K27ac)
     * SAGA组分
     * 转录因子
   - 纯化DNA并通过qPCR或测序(ChIP-seq)分析
   - 绘制修饰和结合的全基因组分布图

3. **转录组分析**
   - 从野生型和突变细胞中提取RNA
   - 制备RNA-seq文库
   - 测序并分析差异基因表达
   - 与ChIP-seq数据相关联，链接组蛋白修饰与基因调控

4. **HDAC抑制剂研究**
   - 用选择性HDAC抑制剂处理细胞
   - 监测组蛋白修饰模式的变化
   - 分析对转录因子结合的影响
   - 评估对基因表达的影响

## 五、数据整合与分析

1. **结构分析和可视化**
   - 使用PyMOL或Chimera生成分子模型
   - 创建显示关键相互作用界面的电子密度图
   - 识别参与底物识别的关键残基
   - 分析底物结合时的构象变化

2. **计算分析**
   - 进行分子动力学模拟
   - 计算静电表面电位
   - 模拟结合相互作用的能量学
   - 预测突变或抑制剂的效果
   - 进行分子对接研究评估小分子结合位点

3. **统计分析**
   - 对生化数据应用适当的统计检验
   - 计算ChIP-seq和RNA-seq结果的显著性
   - 进行结构特征与功能结果之间的相关性分析
   - 验证生物学重复间的可重复性
   - 应用多变量分析识别关键调控因素

4. **数据展示和解释**
   - 创建显示关键界面的结构图
   - 生成比较酶活性的图表
   - 开发ChIP-seq数据的基因组浏览器轨道
   - 将发现整合到转录调控的机制模型中
   - 制作示意图展示修饰如何影响染色质结构

## 六、功能验证实验

1. **体外转录系统**
   - 构建含有模型启动子的DNA模板
   - 准备核提取物或重组转录因子
   - 在有/无修饰核小体存在下进行体外转录
   - 通过引物延伸或RNA测序分析转录产物
   - 评估SAGA复合物和修饰对转录起始和延伸的影响

2. **蛋白质-蛋白质相互作用验证**
   - 进行免疫共沉淀实验验证复合物组分相互作用
   - 使用双杂交或BiFC系统在细胞内验证相互作用
   - 应用FRET或BRET测量活细胞中的动态相互作用
   - 通过体外拉下实验确认直接结合

3. **染色质动力学研究**
   - 使用荧光恢复后漂白(FRAP)研究修饰对蛋白质-染色质结合动力学的影响
   - 应用单分子跟踪技术观察转录因子与修饰染色质的相互作用
   - 进行微核酸酶消化实验评估染色质可及性的变化
   - 使用Hi-C或微C技术研究高级染色质结构改变

4. **药物干预研究**
   - 使用特异性HAT或HDAC抑制剂处理细胞
   - 监测组蛋白修饰水平和分布的变化
   - 分析基因表达改变
   - 将结构见解应用于药物设计和优化

Structural Biology of Histone Modifications and Chromatin Regulation: Experimental Workflow
## I. Sample Preparation and Protein Purification

1. **Recombinant Protein Expression**
   - Clone genes encoding SAGA complex components (e.g., Gcn5, Ada2, Ada3) into expression vectors
   - Transform into E. coli expression strains (BL21(DE3))
   - Induce protein expression with IPTG
   - Harvest cells and prepare lysates under native conditions
   - Purify proteins using affinity chromatography (His-tag, GST-tag)
   - Further purify via ion exchange and size exclusion chromatography

2. **Nucleosome Assembly**
   - Express and purify recombinant histone proteins (H2A, H2B, H3, H4)
   - Refold histone octamers from purified histones
   - Prepare DNA fragments containing nucleosome positioning sequences
   - Reconstitute nucleosomes by salt gradient dialysis
   - Verify assembly by native PAGE

3. **Introduction of Histone Modifications**
   - Chemical synthesis of modified histones
   - Enzymatic modification using purified HATs/HDACs
   - Native chemical ligation to incorporate modified histone tails
   - Verify modifications by mass spectrometry and Western blotting

4. **Complex Formation**
   - Combine purified SAGA complex with modified nucleosomes
   - Optimize buffer conditions for stable complex formation
   - Verify complex formation by gel filtration chromatography
   - Assess stoichiometry by analytical ultracentrifugation

## II. Structural Analysis

1. **Cryo-EM Sample Preparation and Data Collection**
   - Apply 3-4μl of purified complex to glow-discharged EM grids
   - Blot excess liquid and plunge-freeze in liquid ethane
   - Screen grids for ice quality and particle distribution
   - Collect high-resolution data on microscope (e.g., Titan Krios)
   - Record movies with direct electron detector
   - Process data using motion correction software

2. **Cryo-EM Data Processing**
   - Perform CTF estimation and correction
   - Select particles using automated and manual picking
   - Extract particles and perform 2D classification
   - Generate initial 3D model
   - Refine 3D structure through iterative classification
   - Apply post-processing for resolution enhancement
   - Validate resolution using Fourier shell correlation

3. **X-ray Crystallography (for Specific Domains)**
   - Screen crystallization conditions for individual domains
   - Optimize crystal growth parameters
   - Harvest crystals and prepare for diffraction
   - Collect diffraction data at synchrotron facility
   - Process diffraction data and perform phase determination
   - Build and refine atomic models
   - Validate model quality

4. **Model Building and Refinement**
   - Fit atomic models into cryo-EM density maps
   - Refine models against experimental data
   - Validate model geometry and fit to density
   - Analyze protein-protein and protein-nucleosome interfaces

## III. Biochemical and Functional Characterization

1. **In Vitro HAT/HDAC Activity Assays**
   - Prepare substrates (nucleosomes with unmodified histones)
   - Incubate with purified SAGA complex or HDACs
   - Detect acetylation/deacetylation by:
     * Radiolabeled acetyl-CoA incorporation
     * Western blotting with modification-specific antibodies
     * Mass spectrometry analysis
   - Determine enzyme kinetics (Km, Vmax)

2. **Binding Affinity Measurements**
   - Isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC) to measure thermodynamics
   - Surface plasmon resonance (SPR) to measure binding kinetics
   - Fluorescence anisotropy for smaller domain interactions
   - Compare binding affinities for modified vs. unmodified substrates

3. **Mutational Analysis**
   - Design mutations at key interaction interfaces
   - Generate mutant proteins using site-directed mutagenesis
   - Purify mutant proteins and assess structural integrity
   - Measure effects on binding and enzymatic activity
   - Correlate with structural observations

## IV. Cellular and Genomic Studies

1. **Cell Line Engineering**
   - Generate yeast strains with mutations in SAGA components
   - Create human cell lines with CRISPR-Cas9 edited HATs/HDACs
   - Develop cell lines expressing histone mutants (e.g., K→R to prevent acetylation)
   - Verify modifications by Western blotting and mass spectrometry

2. **Chromatin Immunoprecipitation (ChIP)**
   - Crosslink proteins to DNA in living cells
   - Sonicate chromatin to appropriate fragment size
   - Immunoprecipitate with antibodies against:
     * Modified histones (e.g., H3K27ac)
     * SAGA components
     * Transcription factors
   - Purify and analyze DNA by qPCR or sequencing (ChIP-seq)
   - Map genome-wide distribution of modifications and binding

3. **Transcriptome Analysis**
   - Extract RNA from wild-type and mutant cells
   - Prepare RNA-seq libraries
   - Sequence and analyze differential gene expression
   - Correlate with ChIP-seq data to link histone modifications to gene regulation

4. **HDAC Inhibitor Studies**
   - Treat cells with selective HDAC inhibitors
   - Monitor changes in histone modification patterns
   - Analyze effects on transcription factor binding
   - Assess impact on gene expression

## V. Data Integration and Analysis

1. **Structural Analysis and Visualization**
   - Generate molecular models using PyMOL or Chimera
   - Create electron density maps showing key interaction interfaces
   - Identify critical residues involved in substrate recognition
   - Analyze conformational changes upon substrate binding

2. **Computational Analysis**
   - Perform molecular dynamics simulations
   - Calculate electrostatic surface potentials
   - Model energetics of binding interactions
   - Predict effects of mutations or inhibitors

3. **Statistical Analysis**
   - Apply appropriate statistical tests for biochemical data
   - Calculate significance of ChIP-seq and RNA-seq results
   - Perform correlation analysis between structural features and functional outcomes
   - Validate reproducibility across biological replicates

4. **Data Presentation and Interpretation**
   - Create structural figures showing key interfaces
   - Generate graphs comparing enzymatic activities
   - Develop genome browser tracks for ChIP-seq data
   - Integrate findings into mechanistic models of transcriptional regulation

This comprehensive experimental workflow enables researchers to understand the structural basis of histone modifications and their role in transcriptional regulation, providing insights into fundamental epigenetic mechanisms and potential therapeutic targets.
